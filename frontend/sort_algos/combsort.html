Comb sort, a relatively simple sorting algorithm, improves on Bubble sort.

In Bubble sort, when any two elements are compared, they always have a gap of 1. The basic idea of comb sort is that the gap can be much larger than 1. The inner loop of bubble sort, which does the actual swap, is modified such that the gap between swapped elements goes down in steps of a "shrink factor"
